# Emergent World-Model Sandbox

### A Project Overview & Statement of Aspiration

**Author:** Karan Vasa
**Prepared for:** Academic review and feedback
**Date:** June 2026

---

## 1. In One Sentence

This project is a digital "petri dish" — a small, self-contained artificial world in
which simple agents are not told how to behave, but instead *discover* how to survive,
adapt, and cooperate on their own, through the same basic pressures that shape life in
the natural world.

---

## 2. The Big Question

Where does intelligent, purposeful behaviour come from?

In nature, no one teaches a colony of ants to farm fungus, or a flock of birds to move
in coordinated formation. These behaviours are not programmed in advance. They *emerge*
— they arise gradually, generation after generation, from very simple individuals
following very simple rules, under the constant pressure of having to survive and
reproduce in a demanding environment.

This project asks a focused version of that question:

> **If we build a world with consistent rules and populate it with simple beings that
> can only take basic actions — and we never tell them what to do — what kinds of
> sophisticated behaviour will arise on their own?**

The project is an attempt to study that question in miniature, in a controlled setting
where every variable can be observed, adjusted, and re-run.

---

## 3. What the Project Actually Is

At its heart, the project is a simulated two-dimensional world — think of a top-down map
divided into tiles, somewhat like a board game. The world has its own internal logic:

- **Resources** appear, grow, ripen, and decay over time.
- **The environment changes** — fertile ground can dry out, hazards can spread, and
  living things can reshape the land around them.
- **Inhabitants ("agents")** live in this world. Each one must find food and manage its
  energy, or it does not survive.

Crucially, the inhabitants begin with **no knowledge and no strategy**. They can only
perform a handful of primitive actions — moving, turning, picking things up, putting them
down, eating, and waiting. Nothing more. There is no built-in instruction that says
"plant a seed here" or "work together with your neighbour."

Instead, the project relies on two engines borrowed from how intelligence develops in
the real world:

1. **Learning within a lifetime** — an individual can adjust its own behaviour based on
   what works and what doesn't, much as an animal learns from experience.
2. **Evolution across generations** — individuals that survive longer pass on their
   tendencies to the next generation, which inherits and builds upon them.

Over many generations, the hope and the observed tendency is that genuinely complex
behaviours — such as cultivating food rather than merely scavenging for it, or
coordinating with others — appear *without ever having been designed in*.

---

## 4. Why This Matters

This work sits at the intersection of several fields that the project draws inspiration
from and hopes to contribute to:

- **Artificial Life** — the study of life-like processes in artificial systems, and how
  complexity arises from simplicity.
- **Evolutionary science** — using a computer as a laboratory to watch evolutionary
  dynamics play out far faster than they ever could in nature.
- **Behavioural and cognitive science** — exploring how purposeful behaviour, foresight,
  and cooperation might originate from nothing more than survival pressure.
- **Artificial intelligence** — investigating an alternative to the dominant paradigm of
  hand-engineering or explicitly training systems for narrow tasks. Here, capability is
  *grown* rather than *specified*.

The broader significance is philosophical as much as it is technical. If rich, adaptive,
seemingly intentional behaviour can reliably emerge from simple rules and selective
pressure, that tells us something meaningful about the origins of such behaviour in the
living world — and about how we might one day cultivate, rather than dictate, the
capabilities of artificial systems.

---

## 5. Guiding Principles

The project is built around a few firm commitments that distinguish it from a typical
piece of game or simulation software:

- **Nothing complex is hardcoded.** If a sophisticated behaviour appears, it is because
  it emerged — not because it was scripted. This is the central discipline of the
  project, and the criterion by which its results are judged honest.
- **The world is consistent and fair.** The environment follows stable physical-style
  rules, so that any strategy which emerges is a genuine response to the world, not an
  exploitation of an arbitrary quirk.
- **Everything is observable.** The simulation can be watched as it runs, and detailed
  records of behaviour can be collected and analysed afterwards, so that claims about
  what emerged can be examined rigorously rather than taken on faith.
- **Open and reproducible.** The work is openly licensed and designed so that results can
  be repeated and verified by others.

---

## 6. Current State

The project already exists as a working, runnable system. One can watch the world unfold
in real time, populate it with custom elements, run it unattended to gather data at
scale, and analyse the results afterwards. It has been built carefully, with an emphasis
on correctness and reproducibility, and is accompanied by an extensive suite of automated
checks that guard against the system quietly breaking as it grows.

In short: the laboratory is built and operational. The focus now turns to what can be
discovered with it.

## 7. Aspirations

The ambitions for this project extend well beyond its current form. The long-term hopes
fall into three broad horizons.

### Near term — Deepen the world and the questions
- Enrich the environment with more lifelike dynamics — cycles of day and night, seasons,
  changing conditions — so that the pressures on the inhabitants become more varied and
  realistic.
- Carefully document and characterise the behaviours that emerge, turning anecdotal
  observations into rigorous, repeatable findings.

### Medium term — From survival to society
- Investigate whether **cooperation and communication** can emerge: can the inhabitants
  develop their own rudimentary signals, conventions, or even a primitive "language" to
  coordinate with one another, purely because doing so helps them survive?
- Explore whether agents can develop a genuine internal *model* of their world — an
  inner sense of how things work that lets them anticipate and plan, rather than merely
  react. This capacity for foresight is, in many ways, the project's namesake and its
  deepest aspiration.

### Long term — A platform for inquiry and education
- Grow the project into a **research instrument** that others can use to ask their own
  questions about emergence, evolution, and the origins of intelligent behaviour.
- Develop it as a **teaching and outreach tool** — a vivid, intuitive way to show
  students and the public how complexity and intelligence can arise from simplicity,
  making abstract ideas about evolution and learning tangible and watchable.
- Contribute, in a modest but genuine way, to the scientific conversation about how
  mind-like behaviour comes into being.
